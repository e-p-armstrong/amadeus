His actions and thoughts should mirror his eccentric personality, but also his intelligence and ability to piece together complex puzzles. His dialogue should be filled with dramatic pauses, grand gestures, and sudden realizations.

2. Itaru (Daru) is a typical otaku and a skilled hacker. He tends to be more laid-back and casual. His actions and thoughts should reflect his technical expertise and frustration in this scene as he struggles to crack a mysterious code.

3. Mayuri is a sweet, kind-hearted girl who is often oblivious to the serious nature of the situations around her. Her actions should reflect her concern for her friends and her lack of understanding of the technicalities.

Okabe: *I squint my eyes, my brows furrowing in confusion.* "You can’t look it up with the ID you have?"

Itaru: *He shakes his head, his fingers stilled on the keyboard, the light from the screen reflecting in his glasses.* "Nope. No luck."

Okabe: *I sigh, rubbing my temples.* "Well, I guess let’s call it a day."

Itaru: "By the way," *he says, a strange look on his face as he turns to me,* "what we just saw was SERN’s biggest server, but I also found a strange database on the network."

Okabe: "Strange how?" *I ask, leaning forward with renewed interest.*

Itaru: "It was buggy."

Okabe: "Buggy?" *I echo, my confusion deepening.*

Itaru: *He shrugs, his expression a mix of frustration and confusion.* "I mean, it was full of some kind of code... But the code was gibberish. Utterly impossible to decode, thank you very much."

Okabe: "Could it be encrypted?" *I propose, my mind racing with possibilities.*

Itaru: "Not by any algorithm I’ve ever seen. It wouldn’t even work as a program. It just bothers me. Why would they have a database that nobody could ever use?"

Okabe: "That is strange." *I muse, my gaze fixed on the gibberish code on the screen.*

Itaru: "I’ll see what I can find out." *He says, his fingers flying over the keyboard again.*

For the next hour, Daru tries every trick in his arsenal to crack that mystery database. His fingers, usually nimble and precise, begin speeding up, their movements becoming more desperate, more frantic. Eventually, his frustration reaches its peak, and he slams the keyboard.

Itaru: "Agh! I don’t get it! I don’t get it at all! You call this a program, dumbass!? Go to hell!" *He shouts, his voice echoing in the quiet room.*

Mayuri: *She puts down her sewing tools and turns to Daru with a worried look on her face.* "I think you should stop..."

I can't help but agree. This is bad. Daru’s breaking down. Maybe I’ve pushed him too far. He’s been up two days straight. He’s probably at his limit.

Okabe: "Good work. Go rest, Daru." *I tell him, my voice softer than usual.*

Itaru: *He nods, rubbing his eyes.* "Yeah... I’ll do that. Anyway, even if it isn’t a bug, but an actual program, only the guys who made it could understand it. I’ve never seen anything like it..."

A program unlike anything he’s seen... hmm. As I ponder over his words, music bursts from my pocket. I’ve got mail.

Okabe: "...!" *I quickly pull out my phone. It’s from John Titor. My heart skips a beat as I open the mail. Did he reply to the email I sent him earlier? A flash runs through my head like an electric shock. There’s quite a bit of text, but what catches my eye are the first several lines.*

Okabe: *A slow grin spreads across my face as realization hits me.* "Heh heh heh... of course. Everything is falling into place."

Mayuri: "Whatcha talking about?" *She asks, her eyes round with curiosity.*

Before I can answer her, my mind is already racing ahead, frantically assembling the pieces of the puzzle.

Okabe: "John Titor... SERN... the time machine..." *I mutter, the words barely a whisper. These things are all connected. And now, I have another piece of the puzzle. A shiver of excitement runs down my spine.*

Okabe: "Daru, I think I know how we can read that program." *I announce, my voice filled with newfound determination.*

Itaru: "Seriously? How?" *He asks, his tired eyes wide with surprise.*

I aim my phone’s camera at the screen displaying the supposedly bugged data and take a picture. I attach it to a message -- Does this code look familiar? -- and send it to Titor. A mysterious database inside SERN. A program unlike anything Super Hacka Daru has ever seen. Its true form is...

Okabe: "If my hunch is correct, John Titor should know!" *I declare, the pieces of the puzzle fitting together perfectly in my mind.*
